Your Health & Wellbeing

Even if you are busy looking after a loved one, it is important to remember your own needs and take care of your health, emotional and social wellbeing.

How can I take care of my needs and keep healthy?

The Health Innovation Network and Carers Trust worked with carers to develop ‘Time to think about you’ prompts, which can help you think about your feelings, emotions and needs, and whether you want to get some support. This tool can be accessed on the Carers Trust website.

Carers UK have many resources, such as guidance and advice, to help people looking after loved ones to support their own health, keep active and manage complex feelings and relationships. These resources can be access on the Carers UK webpage on looking after yourself.

Carers Trust have also created several resources that can help you take care of your needs and support your wellbeing. Below are the links to some of these resources:

  • Reducing stress
  • Accessing counselling services
  • Tackling loneliness
  • Getting help with sleep

What services can help support my wellbeing?

Carers’ Assessment

Carers’ Assessment is a free service available from the local council to anyone over 18 who cares for another adult. It opens the door to support for carers and provides a space to discuss your caring role and how it affects your life, health, feelings, work and other aspects and what support you need. Carers’ Assessment can be arranged by contacting your local council. You can find more information about it on the NHS website.

Services to help me take a break

The NHS and Carers UK offer advice for carers on taking breaks and explain their importance and benefits. Taking a break from caring is often called ‘respite care’. You can find helpful information on how to arrange respite care on the NHS webpage on carers’ breaks and the Carers UK webpage on taking a break.

Local carer centres and support services

It may be helpful to find out what local support is available to you. Carers Trust offers a tool that you can use to find carer support and services through their UK-wide network of partners. These local carer support centres and charities can provide one-to-one support, respite care, counselling and other services. You can access the tool to search for local carer support on the Carers Trust website.

Support from other carers

You may find it helpful to speak to other people who are experiencing the same things as you and understand the issue carers can face. You can visit the Carers UK to find details of local support groups and online forums. There is no pressure to join in the conversation and you can listen in if preferred.

Getting active

Being physically active can help with your wellbeing and reduce stress. Carers UK offers free tips on getting active and what you can do to increase activity levels and how you can join in on the movement.
