Additional Support & Guidance

Where can I get more advice and support

Taking on caring responsibilities can be tough, and it is important that you feel that you can get the right support. We have created a list of different networks that can support you.

You can get more advice and support via the following networks.

Carer support organisations

If you need further help or advice, you can reach out to the following organisations

  • Carers UK
  • Carers Trust
  • Carers Direct - helpline for carers on 0300 123 1053 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 8pm; Saturday and Sunday 11am to 4pm)
  • Carers Direct - online webchat for carers

Care community support organisations

You can speak to other carers online. There are many people who care for others, you may find it helpful to share experiences and receive support online. You don't even have to join in conversations, but it might help to see what's being said.

  • Carers UK Online Meet Ups
  • HealthUnlocked care community
  • Help & Info - Online Chat Support for Carers | Carers Trust

Telephone helplines and forums

You can find a list of useful social care helplines and forums - NHS (www.nhs.uk)

Charities that can support you

If your loved one has a specific health condition, you may find it helpful to refer to the relevant condition charity to find information and support and learn from experiences of others in a similar position. Below is a list of some of the top condition-specific charities in the UK:

Cancer

  • Cancer Research UK
  • Macmillan Cancer Support | The UK's leading cancer care charity

Dementia / Alzheimer’s

  • Specialist support to families facing dementia | Dementia UK
  • Alzheimer's Society (alzheimers.org.uk)

Parkinson’s

  • Homepage | Parkinson's UK (parkinsons.org.uk)

Stroke

  • Stroke Association | Home

Mental Health

  • Home - Mind

Heart disease

  • Welcome to British Heart Foundation - BHF

Arthritis

  • Arthritis Foundation | Symptoms Treatments | Prevention Tips | Pain Relief Advice

Old age

  • Age UK | The UK's leading charity helping every older person who needs us

Other support

If you need help with money

Caring responsibilities can affect your finances and you may be able to get help to increase your income. Citizens Advice has compiled a list of resources that may be available to you but this can be depending on income, living arrangements, or savings.

Help with money:

  • If you are low income you can get help with paying for prescriptions and other healthcare costs from the NHS low-income scheme
  • You can cut down your household costs, including gas and electricity bills, a free or discounted TV licence from TV Licensing or council tax discounts
  • You can have your contributions towards your state pension covered by the government if you've given up or cut down paid work to care for someone. Visit the Carers UK website to see how you can get advice to help you protect your pension
  • You can get a grant or other financial help from a local charity or trust - Turn2Us has details of charities that might be able to help
