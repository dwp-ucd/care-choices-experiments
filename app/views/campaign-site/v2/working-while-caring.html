Working While Caring

Knowing your rights and entitlements at work like Flexible Working can help you combine your job and care activities if you decide to do so.

What are my legal rights and entitlements that could help me combine work and caring?

Flexible working

If you are working and supporting a loved one with care needs, flexible working may be possible. Flexible working is a way of working that suits an employee's needs, for example having flexible start and finish times, or working from home. All employees have the legal right to request flexible working. You can learn more about flexible working and how to apply for it.

Carers UK have put together some short videos about flexible working which talk about:

  • Where to start with flexible working
  • How to communicate your request
  • Information about your rights

Time off

As an employee, you're entitled to time off to deal with an emergency involving a dependant. A dependant could be a spouse, partner, child, grandchild, parent, or someone who depends on you for care. You can learn more about your rights to time off for family and dependents.

You can also check to see if you can take time off work on the Citizens Advice website.

How can I discuss my situation and available support with my employer?

If you are working and caring for someone, you may find it helpful to discuss your options and workplace policies with your employer. It is always best to tell your employer about your situation and your new responsibilities.

You may consider discussing with your employer (e.g. HR or Line Manager) the following things:

  • If your employer has any policy for carers or a carer support network
  • What adjustments you could make to your role to help you combine work and caretaking responsibilities (e.g. reducing hours, changing patterns, taking extra time off)

If you need specific advice on how to have these conversations, you can:

  • Get support on how to talk about your caring role to your employer on the Carers UK website
  • Get support on how to talk to your employer about family caregiving needs on the Rest Less website
  • Find out more about Carer Passport, which is a record which identifies a carer in some way and sets out an offer of support, services or other benefits in response

Employee support

  • Employers for Carers (EfC) is an employers' membership forum supported by the specialist knowledge of Carers UK. EfC's key purpose is to ensure that employers have the support to retain and empower employees with caring responsibilities. EfC run a benchmarking scheme called Carer Confident, which assists employers to build a supportive and inclusive workplace for employees who are, or will become, carers. For a list of employers who are Carer Confident visit the Employers for Carers website
  • National Careers Service provides personalised careers advice and information.

I am considering leaving work for my caring responsibilities

If you’re considering giving up work to care for someone, it is a big decision and it is important that you consider all options before committing to this. The Carers UK site provides information and guidance on the available support and what to consider.

Finances

  • Before making any decisions, it is therefore a good idea to get a benefits calculator check to see what your financial situation would look like if you were to leave work or reduce your hours.

Pension

  • This may affect your savings in the future in the form of a pension and we explain what options are available to you and suggest ways to ensure you can still contribute to and protect your future pension.
  • You may need to plan ahead and think about your future pension
