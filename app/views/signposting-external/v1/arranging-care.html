Your options and available support for arranging care

There are different options for arranging care, that you, the person you care for, and others involved, may consider based on the care needs and individual circumstances.

Care support, services and equipment

There are ways to get professional care services and other types of support, which may include services and support funded by local authority, paid care and NHS-funded care.

NHS information on care needs assessment

Everyone can receive a needs assessment for free from their local council to get recommendations for services like home care help, disability equipment, home adaptations and day centres.

NHS care after illness or hospital discharge

NHS can provide short-term care (also known as aftercare or reablement) to help people get back to normal after an illness or hospital discharge and stay independent.

Planning and coordinating care

NHS advice for carers on sharing your home

If you care for someone who doesn't live with you, the time may come when you start thinking about sharing your home with them. This might be because they'll need more care in the future if their health gets worse, or they've recently come out of hospital after an illness or fall.
