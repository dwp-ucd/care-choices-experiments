Care Choices

If you are starting to look after a family member or a friend because they are ill, they are an older person or they are disabled, there are resources to support you.

This page guides you through the support that's there for you and the person you care for. You may also find this page useful for planning ahead, if you think you might be regularly caring for someone in the future.

Your options and available support for arranging care

There are different options for arranging care, that you, the person you care for, and others involved, may consider based on the care needs and individual circumstances.

Care support, services and equipment

There are ways to get professional care services and other types of support, which may include services and support funded by local authority, paid care and NHS-funded care.

  • NHS information on care needs assessment
  • NHS care after illness or hospital discharge
  • NHS continuing care
  • NHS information on getting help at home from a paid carer
  • NHS information on care homes
  • NHS-funded nursing care
  • NHS information about home adaptations and home assessment
  • NHS information about household gadgets and equipment
  • NHS information about personal alarms, monitoring systems and key safes
  • NHS information about mobility equipment

Planning and coordinating care

  • NHS advice for carers on sharing your home

If you are working and caring for someone

If you are working and caring for someone, you may find it helpful to understand your employment rights and discuss your options and workplace policies with your employer.

Understanding your rights and options at work

  • Time off for family and dependants
  • Flexible working
  • Learn more about taking time off work on Citizens Advice website

Planning and preparing for the future

  • Get personalised careers advice and information from the National Careers Service
