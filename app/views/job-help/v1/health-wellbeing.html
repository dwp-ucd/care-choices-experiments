Looking after yourself is important and will put you in the best position to work and care for someone else. Take the time to make sure you have enough support. This could include connecting with others in similar situations, taking a break from caring, or exploring local council support.

Find out more:

Coping with difficult emotions

When someone you know needs care, you can feel guilty about making time for yourself or for other activities. Recognising these feelings and learning how others have dealt with challenging care situations can help protect your wellbeing, relationships, and your ability to support the person who needs care.

Get support to cope with guilt and difficult emotions on Carers UK. You can also call Carers Direct on 0300 123 1053 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 8pm; Saturday and Sunday 11am to 4pm) or find other helplines on NHS.UK.

What to do if you’re feeling overwhelmed

There’s lots of people who want to help you, so please reach out for support. For confidential advice and information, call Carers Direct on 0300 123 1053 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 8pm; Saturday and Sunday 11am to 4pm) or find other helplines on NHS.UK.

If you need to speak to someone urgently, you can call the Samaritans at any time on 116 123.
